# Example Playbooks

These playbooks are public-safe examples of institutional-control doctrine. They are not raw internal playbooks and not empirical evidence for the paper.

Use them as templates for AI-staffed work systems that need explicit ownership, role authority, doctrine maintenance, and governed adaptation. Source-specific names, private tools, staffing maps, domain assignments, and sensitive operational details have been generalized.

| Example | What It Demonstrates |
| --- | --- |
| [Organization Manager - Playbook Writing and Maintenance Standard](organization_manager_playbook_writing_and_maintenance_standard.md) | How reusable doctrine is written, maintained, moved, and promoted without losing operational detail. |
| [Organization Manager - Hiring and Employee Role Design](organization_manager_hiring_and_employee_role_design.md) | How role contracts, authority boundaries, tool access, and activation rules stay separate from reusable playbook doctrine and case-specific work records. |

## Redaction Boundary

The examples remove or generalize:

- private organization names;
- employee names;
- private tool names and IDs;
- trading, account, order, venue, and market details;
- staffing maps, location maps, and operating assignments;
- raw work-record, inbox, and ticket references.
